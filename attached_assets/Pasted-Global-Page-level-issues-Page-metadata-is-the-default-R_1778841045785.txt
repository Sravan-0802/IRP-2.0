Content / copy themes to address across the page

Tone consistency. The page mixes formal placement-program language ("Tailored assessments — only what you've studied") with Gen-Z slang ("The Hustler", "The Main Character", "Infinite Aura", "Top 1%"). That's fine as a brand voice, but make sure it's intentional — right now it shifts mid-page. Decide whether the Gen-Z naming is "the brand" or just level labels, and dial the rest of the copy accordingly.
Numbers are your strongest assets. ₹5K–₹15K, ₹25K+, "2 hrs total," "12 hrs," "24 hrs," "100% online," "1st & 2nd year only." Surface these higher (hero trust strip) and reuse them in CTAs ("Unlock ₹25K+ internships →").
Define jargon on first use. "YOG 2028" appears in the hero — first-year students may not know "YOG = Year of Graduation." Add "(Year of Graduation)" the first time.
The phrase "fully merit-based" is great but unsupported. Add a one-liner: "No referrals, no quotas, no fees — your performance decides."
Many sections end with a CTA ("Open Registration Form", "Submit form here", "See Infinite Aura eligibility →"). Audit them so the page has clear primary funnels (Register → NOC → Prepare). Right now CTAs feel scattered.

Visual / UI polish

The hero illustration is sharp; the "What is IRP 2.0?" illustration is also good — but they're in different art styles. Pick one illustration style throughout (both or neither) so the page feels cohesive.
The Level cards use 3 different accent colors (blue / blue / purple). The Learning Path uses blue header bars and a separate purple bar for L3. Standardize: L1 = blue, L2 = teal/green, L3 = purple. Apply that color system consistently to the level cards, learning path tracks, and the "unlocks" pills.
There's a lot of whitespace between major sections, which is fine, but the testimonial section's light-blue background creates a soft transition while other sections jump from white to white with no separator. Add subtle section dividers or background tints to make the page feel structured rather than scroll-heavy.
The avatar style on testimonials (round, dark photos) is heavier visually than the cards around them — match the visual weight.
Some text is light grey on white in body paragraphs (e.g., FAQ answers). Use a slightly darker grey for readability.
